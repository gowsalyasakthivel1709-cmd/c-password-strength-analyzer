Introduction
Encryption and decryption are essential cybersecurity techniques used to protect data from unauthorized access. Encryption converts readable data (plaintext) into an unreadable format (ciphertext), while decryption converts the ciphertext back into its original readable form.
Objectives
Understand the concepts of encryption and decryption.
Learn the importance of data security.
Explore basic encryption methods.
What is Encryption?
Encryption is the process of converting plain text into coded text to prevent unauthorized access.
Example:
Plain Text: HELLO
Encrypted Text: KHOOR
What is Decryption?
Decryption is the process of converting encrypted data back into its original form.
Example:
Encrypted Text: KHOOR
Decrypted Text: HELLO
Types of Encryption
1. Symmetric Encryption
Uses the same key for encryption and decryption.
Faster and commonly used for large amounts of data.
2. Asymmetric Encryption
Uses two keys: a public key and a private key.
More secure for data exchange over networks.
Importance of Encryption
Protects sensitive information.
Ensures data confidentiality.
Prevents unauthorized access.
Secures online transactions and communications.
Applications of Encryption
Online banking
Email security
Cloud storage protection
Secure messaging applications
Advantages
Enhances privacy and security.
Protects data from cyber threats.
Builds trust in digital communications.
Conclusion
Encryption and decryption play a vital role in cybersecurity by protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access. Understanding these concepts helps individuals and organizations maintain data security and privacy.
